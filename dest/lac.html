Los Angeles

California

Method of transport: Car or rental car. Public transport is not advisable in Los Angeles.

Updated on Febuary 20, 2019

Day 1: Art, Beaches, People Watching, and shopping

Part 1: The Getty Museum has one of the most renowned art collections in the World – from impressionist such as Van Gogh to the Renaissance period. It also has beautiful gardens and a picturesque view of downtown LA.

Part 2:After a 20-30 minute drive from the Getty you will arrive at Venice Beach. For shoppers, Abbot Kinney boulevard offers a variety of shops and restaurants; or, shop right along the Venice Boardwalk for quirky outlets and street food.

By far the most popular thing to do is people watch along Venice Beach. You’ll find chiseled bodybuilders at Muscle gym, or adept skateboarders and basketball players at the skatepark and basketball courts. After, relax on the warm sand at one of Cali’s finest beaches.

It is also possible to rent a bike at one of the many outlets along the Venice Boardwalk and cruise over to Santa Monica pier - a year-long carnival along the beachfront.

Day 2: Hollywood - Shopping, museums, & hiking

Part 1:Griffith Observatory has the best view in Los Angeles and is a fantastic place to view the Hollywood sign and catch a sunset! From here it is a 15-20 minute drive to the next destination.

Part 2:A popular walk with lots of interesting shops and venues (such as Jimmy Kimmel, the Hollywood wax museum, candy stores, and the TCL Chinese Theatre) is along the Hollywood Walk of Fame. If you stop by Hollywood and Highland, you can catch a nice view of the Hollywood sign!

Part 3: From here it’s a short drive to Runyan Canyon Park if you’re interested in hiking. You’ll be pleasantly surprised with a nice view of the city, and if you’re lucky, run into a celebrity!

Part 4:For those interested in more shopping – you can take another short (15 minute) drive to Grove Mall and the original farmer’s market. This is the most famous shopping area in Los Angeles! Grove mall boast high end outlets and the farmers market provides a lengthy selection of interesting food stalls.

If Art and design interest you, we recommend the Los Angeles county museum of art and/or Urban light. The entrance fee is $20, and it’s almost walking distance from the Grove.

Day 3: Universal Studios & Universal Citywalk

Part 1:A great day outing is the famed Universal Studios – explore Hogwarts or get the thrill of Fast and Furious – this is a sure way to get your adrenaline going. Ticket price range depends on event selections and can be found at Universal Studios Hollywood

Nearby is the Universal citywalk, which is a great way to shop around, explore restaurants, and see the iconic Hollywood movie outlets.

Day 4: Downtown Los Angeles

The Walt Disney Concert hall is one of the most iconic buildings in modern architecture. For those contemporary art junkies, just a block away is LA’s modern art scene: the museum at the Broad and across is the Museum of Contemporary art.

One of the best food markets in Los Angeles is just a short walk away at the

Grand Central Market

– take your pick from the numerous, delicious, and cheap food vendors. Other interesting nearby sites include the last bookstore, Bradbury building, and the central library.
