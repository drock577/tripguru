Traveling to Vienna

Posted by Kathleen Panek on March 1, 2019

Dear Travelers,

Do you need a new fun place to go? How about Vienna! Vienna is the capital of Austria snuggled in central Europe. Vienna earned the number one stop on Mercer’s most livable cities list. From its majestic Imperial palaces to the Viennese forest that hugs the city, Vienna is a place for everyone. With a population over 1.8 million the city is bustling and there is always something to do. The city is rich with history. A building that was built less than 200 years ago is “new”. Visit Tiergarten Schönbrunn—the world’s oldest continuously operating zoo. Or visit Prater, Vienna’s theme park that originally opened in 1766. One of the historical sights everyone should see is Saint Stephen’s Cathedral—built nearly a thousand years ago--which shines in the heart of the city. The city is known for its culinary delights. Try a Wiener Schnitzel in a Beisl. The traditional Wiener Schnitzel is a thin slice of veal that is then breaded and fried. A Beisl is a typical Viennese dining establishment, nothing fancy, but that is where the real traditional food hides. Don’t forget to try the world’s most famous chocolate cake—the Sachertorte! It is also important to remember that Vienna has 23 districts. Most people get caught up in the first district (the city center) and do not adventure to other districts—each having their own charm. Fun travels!

Your travel expert,

Kathleen Panek
